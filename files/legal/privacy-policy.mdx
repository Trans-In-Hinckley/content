Your privacy and safety are paramount to us. Because our website provides help and advice to trans people, we understand that the information you share, particularly concerning gender identity and related issues, is highly sensitive. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal data.

This website is operated by Trans In Hinckley ("we", "us", or "our"). We are the data controller responsible for processing your personal data.

## 3. How We Store and Protect Your Data

**Security Measures**
We take the security of your data seriously, especially the highly sensitive information submitted via the Safeguarding Form.

1. **Encryption:** All data transferred between your browser and our website is secured using SSL/TLS encryption.
2. **Access Control:** Access to data submitted via the Contact and Safeguarding forms is strictly limited to authorised staff or volunteers who require it to perform their duties.
3. **Digital Storage:** Data is stored on secure, encrypted servers located within the UK/EEA.

**Data Retention**
We only retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes we collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

- **Contact Form Submissions:** Typically deleted after 6 months once the query has been fully resolved, unless a legal obligation requires a longer retention period.
- **Safeguarding Form Submissions:** Due to the critical nature of this data, records may be retained for up to 7 years (or longer if mandated by local safeguarding law) to ensure a complete record exists should official inquiries or legal action take place.
- **Anonymised Analytics Data:** Retained for [e.g., 26 months].

## 4. How We Share Your Data

We will never sell your personal data. We only share data in the following specific circumstances:

### A. Sharing for Safeguarding Purposes (Mandatory Reporting)

If you submit a Safeguarding Form, or if any communication leads us to believe that there is a serious risk of harm to you or another person, we are legally and ethically obligated to share relevant details with the appropriate statutory services. This may include:

- Emergency Services (Police, Ambulance)
- Local Authority Social Services
- Child Protection Agencies

This sharing is strictly limited to the necessary data required to prevent harm.

### B. Service Providers

We use third-party service providers to help us operate the website and handle communications. When you submit a form, your data is processed by the following providers:

- **Cloudflare Workers (Hosting & Edge Computing):** Used to host and deliver the website content and ensure website performance and security.
- **Cloudflare Turnstile (Bot Validation):** Used to verify users submitting forms are human and not automated bots, enhancing security and preventing spam/abuse.
- **Resend (Email Service):** Used for securely transmitting the content of the Contact Form and Safeguarding Form submissions to our designated email addresses.
- **Google Analytics (Website Analytics):** Used to track website traffic, user demographics, and overall usage patterns, helping us understand how people use our service. Data is processed in an anonymized or aggregated manner where possible.
- **Microsoft Clarity (User Behavior Analytics):** Used for session recording, heatmaps, and other visual analytics tools to understand how users interact with the site's interface. This includes capturing mouse movements, clicks, and scroll depth on an anonymized basis.

## 5. Your Data Protection Rights

Under data protection law, you have the following rights:

- **Your right to access** – You have the right to ask us for copies of your personal information.
- **Your right to rectification** – You have the right to ask us to correct information you think is inaccurate.
- **Your right to erasure** – You have the right to ask us to erase your personal information in certain circumstances.
- **Your right to restriction of processing** – You have the right to ask us to restrict the processing of your information in certain circumstances.
- **Your right to object to processing** – You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
- **Your right to data portability** – You have the right to ask that we transfer the information you gave us to another organisation, or to you, in certain circumstances.
  To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at [privacy@transinhinckley.org.uk](mailto:privacy@transinhinckley.org.uk).
